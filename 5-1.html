The Algorithmic Self - Chapter 5.1

Isn't it funny how when you meet someone for the first time, the first thing they do is check your Instagram to figure out who you are?

The beauty of truly getting to know someone- the pauses, the quirks, the stories- gets drowned out by the noise of curated grids and aesthetic snapshots.

So the question becomes: do I really know this person?
At one point, I caught myself asking: am I me, or am I my feed?

Algorithms are reconstructing us, based on what we like, what we wear, even how we speak—exposed to a curated feed that curates different versions of us, shaping a system of not who we are but who we should be. Under this homogenized identity we consume the same aesthetics, repeating the same jokes, vibing to the same dopamine loops as millions of others. Decoded: Designing Authenticity Chapter 5.1 dives into this very question- how much agency do we have to determine our identity and authenticity in this invisible system that's actually exploiting our choices based off our data.
